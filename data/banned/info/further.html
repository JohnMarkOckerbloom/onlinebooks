{pt:Further reading}

Websites

  • Banned Books Week 2023 is observed October 1-7. Find out more at the {ln:https://bannedbooksweek.org/|Banned Books Week Coalition website} and the {ln:https://www.ala.org/advocacy/bbooks/banned|American Library Association website}.
  • The EveryLibrary Institute has Tasslsyn Magnusson's {ln:https://www.everylibraryinstitute.org/book_censorship_database_magnusson|Book Censorship database} tracking book bans and challenges in the United States in 2021 and 2022.
  • PEN America has a {ln:https://pen.org/report/banned-usa-growing-movement-to-censor-books-in-schools/|Banned in the USA} report and data set tracking recent attempts to censor books in schools.
  • The {ln:https://www.bannedbooksbookclub.com/|Banned Books Book Club} features and discusses important books that have been the targets of censorship attempts, including a number of the titles featured in this exhibit. You can also use their site to request free copies of banned titles you are not otherwise able to get, or to fund sending free copies to those who need them.
  • Extensive book bans have long been in effect in many prisons. Books to Prisoners has collected {ln:https://www.bookstoprisoners.net/banned-book-lists/|Prison Banned Books Lists} for many states.
  • The {ln:https://www.library.upenn.edu/|Penn Libraries}, which hosts this website, also has a {ln:https://www.library.upenn.edu/detail/collection/banned-books-collection|Banned Books Collection} featuring original print copies of books published in the US in the 1940s and 1950s that were banned from sale and condemned by authorities for their sexual content.
